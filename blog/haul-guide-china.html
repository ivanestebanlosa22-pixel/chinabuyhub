China Haul Guide 2026: How to Order Multiple Items

If you've ever ordered a single item from China and paid almost as much for shipping as for the product, you already know the problem. The secret experienced buyers discovered years ago is simple: consolidate everything into a haul. A China haul means grouping multiple items from different sellers into a single consolidated shipment, drastically reducing the shipping cost per item and unlocking savings that individual orders can never match.

Whether you're buying streetwear, electronics, collectibles, home goods or niche finds from Taobao, 1688 or Weidian, doing a haul is the most cost-effective way to buy from China. In this complete guide, we'll walk you through every step of the process, from planning your shopping list to receiving a single box at your door.

What is a China Haul?

A China haul is the practice of ordering multiple products from Chinese e-commerce platforms and shipping them together in a single consolidated package. Instead of making ten separate orders and paying international shipping ten times, you order everything to a single warehouse address inside China. The warehouse holds your packages and once everything arrives, they combine them into one box and ship it to you.

The term "haul" originally comes from fashion and shopping culture, where people would show off large shopping collections. In the world of Chinese shopping, it has become the standard method for buying multiple items efficiently.

The haul process is managed through a shipping agent, which is an intermediary service that provides you with a warehouse address in China. You send all your purchases to this address and the agent handles storage, inspection, repackaging and international delivery.

Planning Your Haul

Planning is the difference between a smooth, cost-effective haul and a frustrating experience with unexpected fees.

1

Set Your Budget

Decide how much you want to spend total, including product costs, agent fees, domestic shipping within China and international shipping. A good rule is to allocate about 30-40% of your budget for shipping and fees.

2

Create Your Shopping List

Browse Taobao, 1688, Weidian or other platforms and compile a list of everything you want. Note the item price, seller, estimated weight and link.

3

Choose Your Agent

Sign up with a shipping agent that supports the platforms you're buying from. Compare their fees, shipping lines, warehouse locations and inspection services. Popular choices include USFans, Kakobuy and Litbuy.

4

Place Your Orders

Use your agent's purchasing service or buy directly from sellers using the warehouse address your agent provides. Make sure each package is addressed to your agent's warehouse with your unique member ID.

5

Wait for All Packages to Arrive

Domestic shipping in China typically takes 2 to 5 days. Monitor your warehouse dashboard. Don't rush to ship until every item is accounted for. Most agents offer free storage for 30 to 90 days.

6

Review QC Photos and Request Consolidation

Once all items arrive, the agent takes quality control photos. Review them to ensure everything looks correct and undamaged. When satisfied, select all items and request consolidation.

7

Choose Shipping Line and Pay

Select the international shipping method that fits your budget and timeline. Pay for international shipping and your consolidated haul is on its way. Track your package through the agent's dashboard.

How Consolidated Shipping Works

Consolidation is the core mechanism that makes a China haul possible. First, each seller sends your individual order to the agent's warehouse. Once all your packages arrive, you log into the agent's dashboard and select which items you want to ship together.

The warehouse team opens each package, removes unnecessary packaging and repacks everything into a single optimally-sized box. This repackaging step is critical because it reduces volumetric weight, which directly reduces your shipping cost.

Repackaging

Weight Splitting

Tips to Maximize Savings

  1. Always wait for all items to arrive before shipping. The biggest waste is paying international shipping twice because of impatience.
  2. Remove shoeboxes for sneaker hauls. Boxes add significant volume and weight. Ask to have shoes shipped without the retail box.
  3. Request repackaging to reduce volume. Sellers often ship items in large boxes with excessive padding. The agent removes all unnecessary packaging.
  4. Use sea shipping for non-urgent items. Sea shipping can cost 50 to 70% less than air express. It takes 30 to 60 days.
  5. Shop during sale events. Chinese platforms have massive sales during Singles Day (November 11), 618 (June 18) and Chinese New Year.
  6. Buy from 1688 for the lowest prices. 1688 is the wholesale platform. Prices are often 30 to 50% lower than on Taobao.
  7. Combine orders with friends. If you have friends who also want to order from China, combine everything into a single haul. The international shipping cost gets split.
  8. Check volumetric weight vs actual weight. Carriers charge whichever is greater.
  9. Use coupons and referral bonuses. USFans offers up to €800, Kakobuy up to ¥3,000 and Litbuy up to $500.
  10. Avoid weekends and holidays for ordering. Chinese holidays like Golden Week (October) and Chinese New Year (January/February) cause massive delays.
  11. Choose the right shipping line. Express lines like DHL are fastest but most expensive. EMS offers a good balance.
  12. Declare your package appropriately. Work with your agent on customs declaration. Under-declaring risks confiscation; over-declaring may increase duties.

Weight and Volume Optimization

Understanding how carriers calculate costs is key to optimizing your haul. Carriers use two measurements and charge whichever is greater: actual weight and volumetric weight.

Reduce Actual Weight

  • Remove unnecessary packaging, tags and inserts before consolidation
  • Ship shoes without retail boxes whenever possible
  • Avoid bringing heavy items you can buy locally

Reduce Volumetric Weight

  • Request compression and vacuum sealing for clothing items
  • Ask for items to be packed compactly with minimal empty space
  • Fold or disassemble large items when possible
  • Choose shipping lines that use actual weight instead of volumetric weight

When to Split Your Haul

Sometimes splitting a haul into two smaller packages is cheaper than shipping one large one. Your agent can advise on this when they weigh and measure your consolidated package.

What Not to Haul

Not everything should be bought through a China haul. Some items carry higher customs risk, require special permits or simply aren't worth the shipping cost.

High Customs Risk Items

  • Counterfeit or branded products: Items that imitate well-known brands can be confiscated by customs.
  • Electronics with batteries: Lithium battery restrictions apply to international shipping.
  • Food and supplements: Perishable products face strict import regulations.
  • Cosmetics and skincare: Many countries restrict importing cosmetics from China.

Items That Cost More to Ship Than They're Worth

  • Extremely heavy items like weights, tools or large hardware
  • Very bulky but cheap items like large pillows or foam mats
  • Fragile items like dinnerware or ceramics requiring special packaging and insurance

Legally Restricted Items

  • Weapons, weapon parts or tactical equipment
  • Drugs or controlled substances
  • Live plants or animals
  • Items that violate intellectual property laws

When in doubt, check your country's customs website or ask your agent.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a China haul and how does it work?

A China haul is when you order multiple items from Chinese platforms like Taobao, 1688 or Weidian through a shipping agent. The agent receives all your packages at their Chinese warehouse, then consolidates them into a single shipment and sends it to your address abroad. This saves money on international shipping because you pay for one combined package instead of many individual ones.

How many items should I include in my first haul?

For your first haul, 3 to 8 items is an ideal range. This gives you enough variety to test the process without risking too much money. The cost-efficiency sweet spot is usually 5 to 10 items.

Which agent is best for doing hauls from China?

For hauls, Kakobuy and Litbuy are excellent choices because they offer free storage, consolidated storage and multiple shipping lines. USFans is also a solid option with competitive shipping rates and reliable consolidation services.

How long does a consolidated haul shipment take to arrive?

Shipping times vary by method. Express lines like DHL or FedEx take 5 to 10 business days. Standard EMS or ePacket lines take 12 to 25 days. Economy lines can take 25 to 45 days.

Is there a maximum weight limit for a haul shipment?

Most agents allow individual packages up to 20 to 30 kg. For heavier hauls, the agent may split your items into multiple boxes.

Can I mix different sellers in a single haul order?

Absolutely. That's the whole purpose of a haul. Order from as many sellers as you want, and each seller ships to your agent's warehouse.

What if one of my items arrives damaged at the warehouse?

When an item arrives at the warehouse, staff inspect it and take photos. If you notice damage in the QC photos, you can reject the item and request a refund before it is shipped internationally.

Do I have to pay customs duties on my haul?

This depends on your country's import laws. Many countries have a duty-free threshold. If the declared value of your package is below this threshold, you typically won't pay import duties.
