This guide covers every shipping method, real costs per kg, delivery times to major destinations, customs tips and money-saving strategies. Last updated: June 2026.

Which Method Do Most Buyers Choose?

E-EMS is the most popular shipping method among Chinese agent customers. It offers the best balance of speed (10-18 days) and cost (8-14$/kg). For hauls under 2kg, ePacket provides excellent value at 4-8$/kg. For urgent orders to Europe and North America, DHL Express is worth the premium at 20-28$/kg with delivery in 5-10 business days.

Express Shipping: DHL, FedEx and UPS

Express couriers are the fastest and most reliable way to ship from China. DHL is the most widely used by Chinese shopping agents for express shipping. Packages typically arrive in 5-10 business days to most destinations worldwide. Express shipping includes full real-time tracking, insurance and door-to-door delivery. The main drawback is cost: express rates range from 20-38$/kg, making them 2-3x more expensive than standard options.

DHL is particularly strong for shipments to Europe and the UK. FedEx often provides better rates for shipments to the USA and Canada. UPS is a solid alternative with similar pricing to DHL. Most agents automatically select the carrier with the best rate for your destination. Express shipping is recommended for orders under 2kg where the premium is more justifiable, or for high-value items where speed and insurance matter more than cost.

Standard Shipping: EMS and E-EMS

EMS (Express Mail Service) is China Post's international express service, and E-EMS is an upgraded version offered by most shopping agents with better rates. EMS is the most popular shipping choice among agent buyers because it balances cost and speed effectively. Delivery takes 10-18 business days and costs 8-14$/kg. Tracking is available end-to-end in most countries through the local postal service.

EMS is reliable for packages up to 5kg. For larger hauls, prices scale favorably. Many agents offer "EMS preferential" lines specifically for heavier packages at discounted rates. EMS is slightly slower than DHL but significantly cheaper, making it the default choice for most buyers ordering 2-5 items per shipment.

Economy Shipping: ePacket and SAL

ePacket is a joint venture between China Post and destination country postal services (USPS, Royal Mail, Correos, Canada Post, Australia Post). It is designed for lightweight packages under 2kg and costs 4-8$/kg. Delivery takes 15-25 business days. ePacket is ideal for single-item orders where shipping cost is the priority.

SAL (Sea-Air-Land) is a hybrid method where packages travel partway by sea and finish by air. It costs 5-9$/kg and takes 20-35 business days. SAL is a great choice for large hauls over 3kg where you want to save money but aren't in a hurry. Tracking with SAL can be limited once the package leaves China.

Sea Freight for Large Shipments

Sea freight is the cheapest option at 2-5$/kg but requires patience with delivery times of 30-50 business days. It is only practical for large shipments over 20kg, such as wholesale orders, furniture or bulk goods. Most shopping agents offer sea freight as a custom quote rather than a standard shipping option. Sea freight is commonly used by businesses importing commercial quantities from Chinese suppliers.

Real Cost Example: Shipping a Pair of Sneakers

A typical pair of sneakers weighs 0.8-1.2 kg. Shipping one pair via E-EMS to Spain costs approximately 9-14$ (8-13€). Via DHL Express it costs 24-32$ (22-30€). Shipping 3 pairs together via EMS costs roughly 18-25$ total, saving 40-50% per pair compared to shipping individually. Always consolidate multiple items into one shipment when possible.

How to Save on Shipping from China

Shipping costs can add up quickly, especially for first-time buyers. Here are the most effective strategies to reduce your international shipping costs when using Chinese shopping agents:

1. Consolidate Multiple Items into One Shipment

The single most effective way to save on shipping is to consolidate multiple items into one package. Shipping 1 kg costs approximately 10-14$ via EMS, but shipping 3 kg costs approximately 18-25$ — that's 6-8$ per kg instead of 10-14$ per kg. Most agents offer free warehouse storage for 30-90 days, allowing you to accumulate items from different sellers and ship everything together. Plan your purchases to hit 3-5 kg per shipment for the best value.

2. Choose the Right Shipping Method for Your Package

Selecting the wrong shipping method is the most common money-wasting mistake. Use these guidelines: under 1 kg and urgent — ePacket or DHL Express; 1-3 kg — E-EMS is the best balance; 3-5 kg — EMS or SAL for savings; over 5 kg — SAL or sea freight for maximum value. Most agent dashboards show all available methods with prices, making comparison easy.

3. Use Vacuum Sealing and Remove Packaging

Most agents offer free vacuum sealing for clothing and soft items. Vacuum sealing reduces package volume by 40-60%, and since shipping costs are often calculated on volumetric weight, this directly reduces your cost. You can also request removal of original shoe boxes and retail packaging to reduce both weight and volume. Keep only essential packaging to protect the item during transit.

4. Ship During Promotional Periods

Shopping agents frequently run shipping promotions, especially during Chinese shopping holidays like Singles' Day (November 11), Chinese New Year (January/February) and mid-year sales. During these periods, agents often offer 20-50% discounts on shipping, free upgrades from E-EMS to DHL, or reduced rates on SAL and sea freight. Plan your larger hauls around these promotional windows.

Shipping Cost Trap: Volumetric Weight

Shipping costs are calculated on either actual weight or volumetric weight (length x width x height / 5000), whichever is higher. Bulky but lightweight items like jackets, hoodies and empty boxes cost more to ship than their actual weight suggests. Always request vacuum sealing for such items. A hoodie that weighs 0.5 kg might be billed at 1.5 kg if not compressed.

5. Compare Shipping Rates Between Agents

Different agents negotiate different rates with carriers. USFans has competitive rates for express shipping to Europe. Kakobuy offers lower handling fees (3-4%) which reduces total cost. Litbuy frequently runs shipping promotions. Before committing to a large shipment, check the shipping calculator on each agent's website to compare total costs including service fees.

Most shopping agents allow you to specify the declared value for customs purposes. Agents typically declare values between 15-25$ per package, which keeps shipments under the duty-free thresholds of most countries. Express carriers like DHL are more likely to trigger customs inspections than postal services like EMS or China Post.

Tips to Minimize Customs Issues

Keep individual package values under 20$ declared. Never put more than 3 pairs of sneakers or 5 clothing items in one package. Split large orders into multiple shipments 1-2 days apart. Use EMS or ePacket instead of DHL for lower customs scrutiny. Avoid shipping branded items in their original packaging. If your country charges VAT on imports, factor this into your total cost calculation.

How Shipping Works with Agents

Understanding the step-by-step shipping process helps you make better decisions and avoid costly mistakes. Here is how international shipping works when you buy through a Chinese shopping agent:

1

Purchase Items Through Your Agent

You buy products from Weidian, Taobao or 1688 using your agent's purchasing service. The agent places the order on your behalf with the Chinese seller. The seller ships the item to the agent's warehouse in China. Domestic shipping within China typically takes 2-5 business days and costs 1-3$ (included in your initial payment).

2

Warehouse Processing and QC Photos

Once items arrive at the agent's warehouse, they are inspected and logged into your inventory. The agent takes 3-5 quality control (QC) photos showing the product from multiple angles. You receive a notification to review the photos. This is your last chance to inspect the product before international shipping. If the item is defective, you can request a free return or exchange.

3

Select Items and Choose Shipping Method

After approving QC photos, you select which items in your warehouse to ship internationally. You can choose to ship all items together or split into multiple packages. The agent's dashboard shows available shipping methods, estimated delivery times and costs. You select your preferred method based on speed, cost and reliability.

4

Pay Shipping and Confirm Customs Declaration

You pay the international shipping fee and confirm the customs declaration value. Most agents offer free packaging services including vacuum sealing, bubble wrap and sturdy boxes. You can add insurance (usually 2-3% of declared value) for valuable shipments. Once paid, the agent prepares your package for dispatch.

5

Tracking and Transit

The agent dispatches your package within 24-48 hours of payment and provides a tracking number. Express carriers (DHL, FedEx, UPS) provide detailed real-time tracking. EMS and ePacket tracking updates are available through the carrier's website. Transit times vary by method and destination as detailed in the comparison tables above.

6

Delivery and Customs Clearance

Your package arrives at your local customs office for clearance. Express carriers handle customs clearance as part of their service. Postal services (EMS, ePacket) pass packages to your local post office for final delivery. If customs holds your package, you may need to provide additional documentation or pay import taxes. Sign for your package upon delivery and inspect contents immediately if there are signs of damage.

Shipping from China: Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest shipping method from China?
Sea freight is the cheapest at 2-5$/kg but requires 30-50 days and is only practical for orders over 20kg. For small packages under 2kg, ePacket offers the best value at 4-8$/kg with 15-25 day delivery. For medium hauls (1-5kg), E-EMS at 8-14$/kg is the most cost-effective option balancing speed and price.
How long does shipping from China take to arrive?
Delivery times vary by method: DHL Express 5-10 business days, FedEx 5-10 days, EMS/E-EMS 10-18 days, ePacket 15-25 days, SAL 20-35 days, sea freight 30-50 days. These estimates include 2-4 days warehouse processing time. Express methods are most reliable during peak seasons like before Chinese New Year.
How much does it cost to ship 1 kg from China?
DHL Express: 20-38$/kg. FedEx/UPS: 22-38$/kg. EMS/E-EMS: 8-14$/kg. ePacket: 4-8$/kg (max 2kg). SAL: 5-9$/kg. Sea freight: 2-5$/kg. Prices decrease significantly for heavier shipments — shipping 5kg via EMS typically costs 30-45$ total (6-9$/kg effective rate).
Do I have to pay customs fees when shipping from China?
It depends on your country's threshold: USA (800$), EU (150€), UK (135£), Canada (20 CAD), Australia (1,000 AUD). Most agents declare values between 15-25$ to stay below thresholds. Express carriers (DHL, FedEx) are more likely to trigger customs inspections than postal services. If customs charges apply, you will be notified to pay before delivery.
Which shipping method is best for buying from Chinese agents?
E-EMS is the most popular and recommended choice for most agent purchases. It offers 10-18 day delivery, full tracking in most countries and costs 8-14$/kg. For urgent orders, DHL Express is the best option. For large hauls over 5kg, SAL provides significant savings. Check your agent's specific shipping lines as they vary by destination.
Can I track my package shipped from China?
Yes. DHL, FedEx and UPS provide real-time door-to-door tracking with detailed status updates. EMS provides tracking until delivery in most countries through the local postal service. ePacket provides basic tracking with fewer updates after the package leaves China. Your agent will provide a tracking number within 24-48 hours of dispatch.
What is volumetric weight and how does it affect shipping costs?
Volumetric weight is calculated as (length x width x height in cm) / 5000. Carriers charge based on whichever is higher: actual weight or volumetric weight. For example, a lightweight jacket in a large box might have an actual weight of 0.5 kg but a volumetric weight of 2 kg. Always request vacuum sealing for clothing and soft items to reduce volumetric weight.
What happens if my package gets lost during shipping?
If your package is lost, contact your agent's customer service. Reputable agents offer compensation for lost packages through their insurance or carrier agreements. DHL and FedEx include basic insurance coverage. EMS offers compensation up to a limited amount. For high-value shipments, purchase additional insurance (usually 2-3% of declared value) for full protection.
Can I ship branded items from China?
Yes, but with caution. Many agents accept branded items, but some carriers (especially DHL) may reject packages containing counterfeit luxury goods. EMS and ePacket are less strict. Always declare items honestly and avoid using original brand packaging. If your package is seized by customs, you typically lose the shipment and may face additional consequences depending on local laws.
