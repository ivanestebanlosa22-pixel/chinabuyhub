Common Scams When Buying from China

Understanding the threat landscape is your first line of defense. Below are the most prevalent scam types affecting international buyers in 2026.

  1. Fake Shopping Agents

    Fraudulent websites pose as legitimate shopping agents. They lure buyers with suspiciously low service fees, then disappear with your money after you make payment. These sites often clone the design and branding of real agents, using near-identical layouts and stolen testimonials. Some even run paid ads to appear above legitimate agents in search results.

    ⚠️ Warning

    Never trust a shopping agent you found through a random social media ad. Always navigate directly to the official website by typing the URL yourself or using a verified link from a trusted source.

  2. Bait and Switch ? Cheap Photos, Bad Product

    Sellers on Taobao, Weidian or 1688 post professional product photos that look identical to premium brands. When your item arrives, it is a completely different product ? wrong material, wrong size or a lower-quality alternative from a description that was already low quality. This is extremely common with clothing, sneakers and electronics accessories.

  3. Payment Information Theft

    Unsecured websites without proper SSL certificates that redirect you to suspicious third-party payment pages can capture your credit card details. Some scam agents also store your payment data insecurely, exposing you to identity theft months after your purchase.

    ⚠️ Warning

    Never enter payment details on a website that does not show a padlock icon in the address bar. If a site forces you to pay via a method you have never heard of, walk away immediately.

  4. Non-Delivery Scams

    You pay for an item or service, receive a tracking number that eventually shows "delivered", but nothing ever arrives. The scammer sends a small empty envelope to a nearby address to generate valid tracking, then disputes your complaint by showing proof of delivery. This tactic is becoming increasingly sophisticated.

  5. Quality Bait and Switch

    The product you receive looks like the description but is made with inferior materials. For example, a "genuine leather" bag turns out to be synthetic, or "stainless steel" jewelry tarnishes within days. The seller offers partial refunds (keeping most of your money) or pressures you to accept a defective product because returning it internationally is expensive.

    💡 Pro Tip

    Always request detailed QC (Quality Control) photos through your shopping agent before shipping. A legitimate agent will take multiple photos of your item so you can verify quality and accuracy before it leaves China.

How to Verify a Shopping Agent

Before committing your money to any shopping agent, go through this verification checklist. Skipping even one step can leave you exposed.

1

Check Company Registration and Legal Information

Look for a registered business address, company registration number and verifiable legal entity name. Legitimate agents are transparent about who they are. Look up the company in Chinese business registries or ask for their business license directly.

2

Read Independent Reviews ? Not Just Site Testimonials

Check Reddit, Trustpilot and Discord communities. Look for patterns: consistent complaints about the same issue are red flags. Be wary of agents that only show reviews on their own website ? those can be fabricated.

3

Check SSL Certificate and Website Security

The site must use HTTPS (look for the padlock icon). Click the padlock to see the certificate details ? it should be issued to the company's real name, not a generic provider. Free SSL certificates are common, but the certificate issuer and domain age matter.

4

Test Customer Service Responsiveness

Send a question before signing up. How quickly do they respond? Is the answer helpful and specific, or generic and evasive? Test them on multiple channels ? email, live chat and WhatsApp/Discord if available. A response time under 24 hours is a good sign.

5

Start with a Small Test Order

Never place a large order with a new agent. Start with a single low-value item to test the entire process: ordering, QC photos, shipping and delivery. This small investment can prevent a much larger loss.

6

Check Payment Methods Offered

Legitimate agents offer multiple secure payment options including PayPal and credit cards. If an agent only accepts direct bank transfer, cryptocurrency or obscure payment methods, that is a major red flag. Secure payment options give you buyer protection.

Red Flags to Watch For

Trust your instincts, but also trust these warning signs. If you spot two or more of these on a single site, proceed with extreme caution or avoid it entirely.

🚩 Price Too Good to Be True

If an agent charges significantly less than everyone else for the same service, question why. Sustainable businesses have overheads. Extremely low fees often mean hidden costs, stolen data or an exit scam in progress.

🚩 No verifiable history or domain age

Use WHOIS lookup tools to check when the domain was registered. A site that has only been around for a few months and claims thousands of happy customers is suspicious. Established agents have domain histories spanning years.

🚩 Pressure tactics and urgency

"Limited time offer — 90% off service fees today only!" Scammers create artificial urgency to stop you from doing proper research. Legitimate businesses do not need to pressure you into rushed decisions.

🚩 Inconsistent branding and poor grammar

Check for logos that look slightly wrong, color schemes that do not match across pages and text full of awkward expressions. While minor grammar issues are normal for non-native English sites, widespread inconsistencies suggest a cloned or hastily assembled website.

🚩 No physical address or contact phone

A legitimate business has a physical presence. If the only contact method is a web form or a free email address (Gmail, Outlook), treat it as a red flag. Real agents provide business addresses, phone numbers and multiple communication channels.

🚩 Refusal to provide QC photos

Any agent that will not take photos of your items before shipping is either running a scam or does not care about quality control. QC photos are standard practice among reputable agents. Refusal is unacceptable.

How to Protect Your Payment

Your payment method is your last line of defense. Choose it wisely and follow these practices.

💳

Use Only PayPal or Credit Cards

Both offer buyer protection programs. If a seller or agent does not deliver, you can file a dispute and potentially recover your funds. Never pay via direct bank transfer, Western Union or cryptocurrency to any entity you have not extensively verified.

🔐

Enable Two-Factor Authentication

Protect your shopping agent account and email with 2FA. If a scammer gets your password, 2FA is the barrier that stops them from emptying your account balance or accessing your personal information.

📧

Use a Dedicated Email Address

Create a separate email for buying from China. This limits exposure if the agent's database is compromised and helps you keep all transaction records organized in one place for easier dispute resolution.

🧾

Save Every Receipt and Screenshot

Keep copies of order confirmations, payment receipts, QC photos, chat logs and shipping details. If a dispute arises, this documentation is your most powerful tool. Store them in a cloud folder for easy access.

🚫

Never Share Full Card Details Directly

If an agent asks you to email your full card number, CVV or bank details ? refuse. Payment should always go through a secure payment gateway. No legitimate agent needs your raw card information.

What to Do If You Have Been Scammed

Time is critical. Follow these steps immediately to maximize your chances of recovery.

  1. Document Everything Immediately

    Take screenshots of the website, your order confirmation, payment receipts, chat messages and any tracking information. Save URLs, email addresses and transaction IDs. Do this before the scammer has a chance to alter or delete evidence.

  2. Contact Your Payment Provider

    File a dispute with PayPal or initiate a chargeback with your credit card company. Most providers have strict time windows ? PayPal allows 180 days, credit card chargebacks generally must be filed within 60-120 days. Act fast.

  3. Report to Relevant Authorities

    File a report with your country's consumer protection agency. Also report the scam to the Internet Crime Complaint Center if you are in the US, or your local equivalent.

  4. Warn the Community

    Post your experience on Reddit, Trustpilot and relevant Discord communities. Your warning can prevent others from being victimized. Include screenshots and detailed information about the scam pattern.

  5. Monitor Your Accounts

    If you shared personal information or card details, monitor your bank statements closely for unauthorized transactions. Consider placing a fraud alert on your credit report and changing passwords on all related accounts.

  6. Consider Professional Recovery Services

    For large amounts, professional fund recovery services may be worth it. Research them carefully ? some recovery services are themselves scams. Only use services with verifiable track records and transparent fee structures.

📌 Important Reminder

Prevention is always better than recovery. Taking 15 minutes to verify an agent before placing an order is infinitely better than spending weeks trying to recover lost funds. Invest the time upfront.
