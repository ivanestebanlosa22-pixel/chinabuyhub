Express Courier: DHL, FedEx and UPS

Express courier services are the gold standard for international shipping from China. Companies like DHL, FedEx and UPS operate their own fleets of cargo aircraft, maintain regional sorting hubs, and employ dedicated customs clearance teams in every major market. This vertical integration is what allows them to deliver packages from a warehouse in Shenzhen to a doorstep in Madrid in as little as three days.

Speed

DHL Express is the most used courier for shipments originating from China. Standard DHL Express delivers to Spain in 3-5 business days, to the UK in 2-4 business days, to Canada in 4-6 business days, and to Australia in 3-5 business days. FedEx International Priority offers comparable speeds. UPS Worldwide Express is slightly slower with 4-7 business days for most destinations.

Cost

Express courier shipping is the most expensive option. Rates are calculated based on actual weight or volumetric weight (whichever is greater), using the formula: Length x Width x Height (cm) / 5000. For a 1 kg package to Spain, expect to pay approximately 25-40$ for DHL. Heavier packages benefit from lower per-kilogram rates ? a 10 kg package may cost 12-18$ per kilogram. Volume shippers with negotiated contracts can achieve even lower rates.

Tracking

Tracking with express couriers is exceptional. DHL provides real-time updates with scan events at every transit point ? pickup, export customs, hub sorting, import customs, out for delivery, and final delivery. You can track your package directly on the DHL website or through your agent's platform. FedEx and UPS offer similar granularity.

Customs Handling

Express couriers handle customs clearance as part of their service. DHL, FedEx and UPS have in-house customs brokerage teams that process documentation, calculate duties, and arrange payment. You'll typically receive notification before customs charges are applied, and duties can often be paid online. This is a significant advantage over postal methods, where you may need to visit a post office to handle customs clearance in person.

Note: DHL and FedEx may charge a brokerage fee (5-15$) in addition to actual customs duties. Factor this into your total shipping cost comparison.

E-EMS / EMS (China Postal Express Service)

EMS is an international express postal service offered by national postal operators. In China, EMS is operated by China Post and offers two tiers: standard EMS and the faster E-EMS. Both are significantly cheaper than DHL while still offering respectable delivery speeds and tracking.

Speed

Standard EMS delivers to Spain in 10-15 business days, to the UK in 7-12 business days, to Canada in 10-15 business days, and to Australia in 8-12 business days. E-EMS is faster, typically cutting 2-4 days off these estimates. During peak seasons (November-January, Chinese New Year), add 3-7 additional days to these timelines.

Cost

EMS is considerably cheaper than express couriers. A 1 kg package to Spain costs approximately 15-22$ via EMS. Rates decrease significantly with weight ? a 5 kg package averages 8-12$ per kilogram, and a 10 kg package can cost as little as 6-9$ per kilogram. EMS uses the same volumetric weight calculation as couriers, so lightweight bulky items may incur higher charges.

Tracking

EMS provides tracking through the China Post system and often through the destination country's postal service too (e.g., Correos in Spain, Royal Mail in the UK). Updates are provided at major transit points but are less frequent than with express couriers. You'll typically see scans for: item accepted, item exported, item in transit, item arrived in destination country, item held in customs, and item delivered.

Customs Handling

EMS shipments go through customs as regular postal items. In Spain, packages valued under 150? are generally exempt from duties under the EU de minimis threshold. In the UK, customs may be assessed at the point of delivery, and you may need to pay duties directly to the postal service or customs authority. EMS does not provide integrated customs brokerage like DHL does.

Pro tip: Many agents offer EMS as the default shipping method for medium-weight packages (1-10 kg) because it provides the best balance of speed and cost for most buyers.

ePacket

ePacket was created as a joint initiative between China Post and international postal operators to facilitate cross-border e-commerce. It is specifically designed for lightweight packages up to 2 kg and has become the most popular shipping method for small items ordered from Chinese e-commerce platforms.

Speed

ePacket delivery times vary significantly by destination. To Spain, expect 10-20 business days. To the UK, 12-20 business days. To Canada, 15-25 business days. To Australia, 10-18 business days. The wide range reflects reliance on local postal networks for last-mile delivery, which can vary in efficiency.

Cost

ePacket is one of the most affordable tracked shipping options from China. A standard 500g package to Spain costs approximately 3-5$. Packages up to 1 kg typically cost 5-8$, and the maximum 2 kg package costs 8-12$ depending on destination. There is a minimum charge that applies to very light items, so a 100g package costs about the same as a 300g one.

Tracking

ePacket includes tracking through the China Post system. You'll receive updates when the item is accepted, when it leaves China, and when it arrives in the destination country. However, tracking updates within the destination country may be limited ? in Spain, Correos may not provide detailed scans beyond "in transit" until the final delivery scan. This is generally acceptable for most buyers but can be frustrating if you want precise location information.

Customs Handling

ePacket shipments are processed through standard postal customs channels. In Spain, the EU 150? de minimis threshold means most ePacket packages arrive duty-free. In the UK, customs may apply, and you may receive a card from Royal Mail or your local postal service asking you to pay import charges before delivery.

Best practice: If you're ordering multiple small items, ask your agent to consolidate them into a single ePacket shipment to maximize the 2 kg weight limit and reduce shipping costs per item.

Economy SAL (Surface Air Lifted)

SAL, which stands for Surface Air Lifted, is an economy shipping method offered by China Post. The concept is simple: packages are transported by air when space is available at reduced rates, and by surface (sea) when air capacity is full. This hybrid approach allows SAL to offer lower prices than standard airmail without being as slow as pure sea freight.

Speed

SAL is one of the slowest trackable methods, with delivery times of 15-30 business days to most destinations. To Spain, expect 18-30 days. To the UK, 15-25 days. To Canada, 20-35 days. To Australia, 15-25 days. The variability comes from the unpredictable nature of air capacity allocation ? during busy periods, more packages are routed by sea, extending delivery times.

Cost

SAL is significantly cheaper than EMS and ePacket. A 1 kg package to Spain costs approximately 4-7$. Heavier packages see even better rates ? a 5 kg package averages 3-5$ per kilogram. SAL is often the default shipping method from agents when you select the cheapest tracked option at checkout.

Tracking

SAL tracking is limited. You'll receive a tracking number and updates when the item is accepted and when it leaves China. However, updates during transit and within the destination country are often sparse or non-existent. Many buyers report their SAL packages show "dispatched from origin" and then nothing until "out for delivery." This is normal for economy postal services.

Customs Handling

Like other postal methods, SAL shipments go through standard customs processing. The same de minimis thresholds apply (Spain: 150?, UK: 135?, EU: 150?). Since SAL is often used for lower-value items, many packages pass through customs without any duty charges.

Sea Freight

Sea freight is the oldest and cheapest method of transporting goods from China to the rest of the world. For individual buyers using package agents, sea freight is available through consolidated shipping services that pool multiple small shipments into container loads. It's the preferred option for bulk orders, heavy items, and shipments where time is not a factor.

Speed

Sea freight is by far the slowest shipping method. Transit times from China to Spain are typically 30-45 days. To the UK, expect 30-40 days. To Canada, 30-45 days. To Australia, 15-25 days (shorter due to geographic proximity). These times do not include customs processing or last-mile delivery, which can add 3-10 additional days.

Cost

Sea freight is dramatically cheaper than air methods. Rates typically range from 2-5$ per kilogram for consolidated shipments, with minimum charges of 20-50$ per shipment. For bulk orders of 100+ kg, rates can go below 1$ per kilogram. This makes sea freight the only practical option for shipping heavy items like furniture, bulk electronics, or wholesale merchandise.

Tracking

Sea freight tracking is minimal. You'll typically receive a booking number and occasional updates when the container is loaded and when it arrives at the destination port. Once the shipment is handed off to the local postal or freight service, tracking becomes even more limited. For most buyers, the tracking experience is: "your shipment has been dispatched" followed by a long silence until the delivery notification.

Customs Handling

Sea freight shipments are subject to import duties and taxes, which are typically higher than for postal shipments because they often involve commercial quantities. You'll likely need to work with a customs broker or your freight forwarder to clear the goods through customs. This is an additional cost and administrative burden that makes sea freight impractical for small personal orders.

When to use sea freight: If you're ordering 50+ kg of goods, buying wholesale products for resale, or shipping heavy items like shoes in bulk, sea freight can save you 70-85% compared to express courier shipping.

Which Shipping Method Should You Choose?

Use the decision guide below to quickly determine the best shipping method for your order. Consider your priorities: speed, budget, and package weight.

Scenario 1: I need it now (3-7 days)

Choose DHL Express, FedEx or UPS. This is the only option if your delivery is urgent. Expect to pay 15-25$ per kilogram, but you get full tracking, priority customs clearance, and guaranteed delivery windows.

Scenario 2: I want fast shipping at lower cost (7-15 days)

Choose E-EMS or EMS. These methods offer a great balance of speed and cost. EMS is the most popular option for packages in the 1-10 kg range and is what most agents recommend by default.

Scenario 3: I'm ordering a small, lightweight item (under 2 kg)

Choose ePacket. It's the most cost-effective tracked method for lightweight packages. Delivery takes 10-20 days, and you get basic tracking throughout the journey.

Scenario 4: I want the cheapest option and can wait (15-30+ days)

Choose Economy SAL. At 3-5$ per kilogram, SAL is the cheapest tracked postal option. Ideal for items where you're not in a hurry and want to minimize shipping costs.

Scenario 5: I'm ordering in bulk (50+ kg)

Choose Sea Freight. The cost savings are dramatic ? 2-5$ per kilogram versus 12-25$ for express courier. The trade-off is a 30-60 day wait, but for wholesale orders, this is the industry standard.

Scenario 6: I'm ordering heavy items (shoes, electronics, etc.)

For packages over 5 kg, consider EMS for the best balance. If you can wait 20+ days, SAL will save you a significant amount on heavy shipments. Avoid ePacket, which is limited to 2 kg and becomes expensive at higher weights.

Tips to Save on Shipping

Shipping costs can add up quickly, especially for international orders from China. Here are practical tips to reduce your shipping expenses without sacrificing too much convenience:

  1. Consolidate your orders. Instead of shipping five separate 1 kg packages, combine them into a single 5 kg shipment. You'll pay significantly less per kilogram on the combined package. Most agents offer free warehouse storage for 30-90 days, giving you time to accumulate items before shipping.
  2. Remove original packaging. Ask your agent to remove unnecessary retail boxes, packaging inserts, and air fillers. This reduces both actual weight and volumetric weight, directly reducing your shipping cost.
  3. Choose the right method for the weight. ePacket is cheapest under 2 kg. EMS is more cost-effective between 2-10 kg. SAL saves money for packages over 5 kg if you can wait. Express courier only makes sense for urgent or very high-value items.
  4. Avoid peak season shipping. Rates increase 15-30% during November-January (holiday season) and Chinese New Year (January-February). If possible, schedule non-urgent shipments during off-peak months (March-October).
  5. Declare accurately but strategically. Undeclaring package values is illegal and can result in confiscated packages. However, you should declare the actual value of goods to qualify for de minimis thresholds (e.g., 150? in the EU) and avoid unnecessary customs charges.
  6. Compare agent shipping rates. Different agents negotiate different rates with carriers. Before placing an order, compare shipping quotes from ChinaBuyHub, Kakobuy and Litbuy to see which offers the best deal for your specific shipment.
  7. Use volumetric weight wisely. Express couriers charge by volumetric weight (L x W x H / 5000). If you're ordering lightweight but bulky items, consider disassembling or folding them to reduce dimensions. For example, foldable shoes can save 30-50% on volumetric weight charges.
  8. Ship during sales events. Some agents offer discounted or subsidized shipping during promotional periods. Keep an eye on your agent's announcements for shipping deals, especially around major Chinese shopping festivals like 11.11 and 6.18.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest shipping method from China?

The cheapest shipping method from China is sea freight, which can cost as little as 2-5$ per kilogram for large bulk orders. For smaller packages, China Post's economy SAL and ePacket are the most affordable options, typically ranging between 3-8$ per kilogram depending on destination and package weight.

How long does ePacket take to deliver?

ePacket delivery typically takes 10-20 business days to most destinations. During peak seasons like Chinese New Year or Black Friday, delivery can extend to 25-30 business days. ePacket includes tracking and is available for packages up to 2 kg.

Is DHL faster than EMS for shipping from China?

Yes, DHL is generally faster than EMS. DHL typically delivers in 3-7 business days worldwide, while EMS takes 7-15 business days depending on destination. DHL also offers more reliable tracking and priority customs clearance, but at a higher cost.

Do I have to pay customs duties when shipping from China?

Yes, most countries impose customs duties on products imported from China. In Spain, packages valued under 150? are generally exempt from duties. In the UK, the threshold is 135?. In the EU, duties typically apply above 150?. Express couriers like DHL usually handle customs clearance for you and may charge a brokerage fee, while postal methods may require you to pay duties on delivery.

Which shipping method has the best tracking?

DHL, FedEx and UPS offer the most detailed real-time tracking with frequent scan updates. EMS provides decent tracking with updates at major transit points. ePacket and China Post registered airmail offer basic tracking. Economy SAL and China Post unregistered mail have limited or no tracking once the package leaves China.

Can I use sea freight for small orders from China?

Sea freight is generally not economical for small orders under 30 kg. It is more suitable for large orders of 100 kg or more. For small orders, air methods like ePacket, EMS or express courier services are more practical and cost-effective.

What is SAL shipping from China?

SAL (Surface Air Lifted) is an economy shipping method offered by China Post. It combines air and surface transportation to reduce costs. SAL is cheaper than standard airmail but slower, typically taking 15-30 business days for international delivery. It includes basic tracking and is suitable for lightweight non-urgent packages.
