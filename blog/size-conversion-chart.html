Why Chinese Sizes Are Different

Understanding why Chinese sizes differ from Western systems helps you make better purchasing decisions. Here are the key reasons:

  • Metric system: Chinese sizes are fundamentally built around centimeters. Shoe sizes correspond directly to foot length in cm (e.g., CN 40 = 25 cm foot length). Clothing sizes reference actual body measurements rather than arbitrary numbered scales.
  • Different body proportion standards: Chinese size charts are designed based on average body proportions of the Chinese population, which may differ from Western averages. This affects how garments are cut and proportioned.
  • Manufacturing variation: China is the world's largest manufacturing hub, and products come from thousands of different factories. Each factory may have slightly different sizing standards, even when producing for the same brand.
  • No universal standard: While Western sizes (US, EU, UK) have more standardized systems across regions, Chinese sizes can vary between sellers, brands, and product categories. An L from one Taobao seller may fit like an M from another.
  • Listed vs. actual sizes: Some sellers list their own proprietary sizes that may not accurately reflect standard Chinese measurements. Always trust the specific measurements (in cm) provided in the product listing.
Pro Tip: Never rely solely on the size label (S, M, L, XL) when buying from Chinese sellers. Always check the actual measurements in centimeters provided in the product description. A "Large" from one seller can be completely different from a "Large" from another.

How to Measure Your Size

Getting accurate measurements is the most important step in finding the right size when buying from China. Follow these steps to measure yourself correctly:

1
Measure Your Foot Length

Place your bare foot on a piece of paper against a wall. Mark where your longest toe ends. Measure the distance from the wall to the mark in centimeters. Do this for both feet ? use the larger measurement. This number corresponds directly to CN shoe sizes.

2
Measure Your Chest / Bust

Wrap a flexible measuring tape around the widest part of your chest (for men) or bust (for women), keeping the tape parallel to the floor. Stand naturally and breathe normally. For women, wear the bra you plan to use with the garment for the most accurate measurement.

3
Measure Your Waist

Find your natural waist, which is typically the narrowest part of your torso, just above the belly button. Wrap the measuring tape around this point, keeping it snug but not tight. Don't suck in your stomach ? measure in your natural, relaxed state.

4
Measure Your Hips

Stand with your feet together and wrap the measuring tape around the widest part of your hips and buttocks. Keep the tape level and parallel to the floor. This measurement is particularly important for women's pants, skirts and dresses.

5
Measure Your Inseam

For pants and trousers, measure from the crotch seam to your desired length (usually the ankle or top of the shoe). You can also measure a well-fitting pair of pants by laying them flat on a surface and measuring from the crotch to the hem along the inside leg seam.

6
Compare with the Seller's Chart

Always compare your measurements with the specific size chart provided by the seller in the product listing. Don't assume that an L from one seller will be the same as an L from another. Match your measurements in cm with the seller's ranges for the best fit.

Measuring Tip: Ask someone to help take your measurements for the most accurate results. If measuring yourself, use a mirror to ensure the tape is level and properly positioned. Record all measurements in centimeters to match Chinese sizing standards.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know my China shoe size?

Measure your foot length in centimeters from heel to toe. Chinese shoe sizes correspond directly to foot length in cm. For example, if your foot measures 25 cm, your CN size is 40. Add 1-1.5 cm to your actual foot length for comfort if you prefer a looser fit. Use our conversion chart above to cross-reference with US, EU and UK sizes.

Are Chinese sizes the same as US sizes?

No, Chinese sizes are not the same as US sizes. Chinese sizes use a metric system measured in centimeters, while US sizes use a different numbering scale. For shoes, CN sizes are based on foot length in cm. For clothing, CN sizes are generally one or two sizes smaller than their US equivalents. Always check actual measurements rather than just the size number.

What size should I order on Weidian?

When ordering on Weidian, always check the specific size chart provided by the seller, as sizes vary between manufacturers. Use our CN to US conversion chart as a general starting point, but prioritize the measurements listed in the product description. Read reviews from other buyers to see if items run large or small. Many Weidian sellers include detailed measurements in centimeters for each size ? use those as your primary reference.

Do Chinese clothing sizes run small?

Yes, Chinese clothing sizes generally run smaller than Western sizes. A Chinese XL can fit like a US M or even S, depending on the brand and seller. This is because Chinese sizes are based on average body proportions of the Chinese population, which tend to be smaller than Western averages. Always check the specific measurements (chest, waist, length) provided by the seller and compare them to your own measurements. When in doubt, size up one or two sizes.

How do I convert EU shoe size to China?

To convert EU shoe size to China size, the easiest method is to measure your foot length in centimeters. CN shoe sizes are directly based on foot length in cm (e.g., 25 cm = CN 40). EU sizes use a different formula. As a general rule, CN and EU size numbers are usually very close or identical for the same foot length. Use our conversion chart above to find the exact match of your EU size.

Are Taobao size charts accurate?

Taobao size charts are generally accurate for that seller's specific product, but sizes can vary significantly between different sellers and brands. The key is to look at the detailed measurements in centimeters rather than just the size number (S, M, L). Read customer reviews, look for photos of the product being worn, and if possible, message the seller with your measurements for a personalized recommendation.

What is the difference between CN and JP shoe sizes?

CN (China) and JP (Japan) shoe sizes are very similar since both systems are based on foot length in centimeters. In most cases, CN and JP sizes are interchangeable. However, there may be minor differences in how brands round their sizes or adjust for width. Generally, if you know your size in one system, it will be the same or very similar in the other.

How do I measure my chest for Chinese clothing?

To measure your chest for Chinese clothing, wrap a flexible measuring tape around the widest part of your chest, keeping it parallel to the floor. Stand naturally, breathe normally, and don't puff out your chest. For the best fit, measure over the type of underwear you plan to wear with the garment. Compare your measurement to the seller's size chart in centimeters for the most accurate size match.
